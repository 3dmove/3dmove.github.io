Shin Angle Change: the what, why and how

2025年10月27日 阅读时间：2 分钟
Wayne

Wayne's Blog • Movement • Shin Angle Change

Shin angle change plays an important role in running and jumping. If we can practice it intentionally, our movement may be smoother.

What 链接到标题

Shin angle refers to the angle between the shin and the ground. Shin angle change refers to the motion of the shin rotating forward to the ground.

Why 链接到标题

Why should we care about shin angle change? Think about running. When one leg is on the ground, the shin must get out of way to allow the femur to push the torso forward. One possible problem is that when we run, our shin may barely move forward. Another possible problem is that, when we run, for the leg on the ground, if the shin rotates forward and then the shin rotates backward, which pushes the femur backwards, we will bound up, which slows us from moving forward.

How 链接到标题

The tibia (bone of the shin) and foot are connected in the ankle joint. First, the foot should anchor to the ground. Second, we should allow the tibia rotates forward around the ankle joint. Try this:

  • Start with the athletic posture. Hip sits back. Knees flex. Relax the torso.
  • Rotate the shin forward. Feel your body weight rolls over your feet. Make sure you allow the heel off the ground.
  • Keep rotating the shin towards the ground. At some point, your brain will save you from falling on your face by catching you.
  • When you are comfortable about the sense of falling, try this. Shin angle change until you feel like being about thowning away. Hold the shins (not push foot or knee), allow the femur pushes your torso forward and starts running.

All these things are my understanding of movement learned from Adarian Barr.
