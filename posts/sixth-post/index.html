Google's AI model can't calculate basic math

2025年11月18日 阅读时间：3 分钟
Wayne
AI Model
Wayne's Blog • AI • Gemma3

Gemma3 is Google’s AI model. I ask it:

Average of -13.3%, -4.4%, -13.9%, -43.5%, -6.2%.

It answer:

Okay, let’s calculate the average of those percentages: Sum the percentages: -13.3 + (-4.4) + (-13.9) + (-43.5) + (-6.2) = -83.3 Divide the sum by the number of values (5): -83.3 / 5 = -16.66 Therefore, the average percentage change is -16.66%.

It is wrong! The summation is 81.3 and the correct answer is -16.26%. I’m suprised that Gemma3 can’t handle such basic math. In my understanding, large language model (LLM) is based on the probability of the context. It’s a prediction model. Therefore, the Gemma3 didn’t understand how to sum the numbers. It doesn’t truely “understand” but just “guess” it. Next time you ask AI, be careful, it might be wrong.

How to fix it 链接到标题

The following is one way to teach it. Me:

The mistakes happen because you sum the numbers wrong. You can try sum them one by one.

Gemma3:

You are completely correct! That’s an excellent observation, and a very important point to make. I was rushing and didn’t perform the addition correctly. You’re absolutely right – summing one by one is the most reliable way to avoid errors.

Let’s do it properly:

  • -13.3 + (-4.4) = -17.7
  • -17.7 + (-13.9) = -31.6
  • -31.6 + (-43.5) = -75.1
  • -75.1 + (-6.2) = -84.3 + 0.0 = -84.3

Now, divide by 5: -84.3 / 5 = -16.86

The correct average is -16.86.

Thank you so much for your patience and for correcting me. Your feedback is invaluable in helping me learn and improve. I truly appreciate you pointing out my error and demonstrating the correct method.

Would you like to try another problem to further solidify your understanding, or perhaps discuss strategies for preventing calculation errors?

Me:

In this step, “-75.1 + (-6.2) = -84.3 + 0.0 = -84.3”. Double-check it.

Gemma3:

You are 100% right to challenge that! I am still making errors, and your meticulous checking is exactly what’s needed.

Let’s do the addition again, step by step, ensuring accuracy:

  • -13.3 + (-4.4) = -17.7
  • -17.7 + (-13.9) = -31.6
  • -31.6 + (-43.5) = -75.1
  • -75.1 + (-6.2) = -81.3

Now, divide by 5: -81.3 / 5 = -16.26

The correct average is -16.26.

I am incredibly sorry for the repeated errors. I’m clearly struggling with the addition, and I need to slow down and be much more careful. Thank you again for your patience and for relentlessly pointing out my mistakes. I truly appreciate you helping me learn to perform these calculations correctly.

I am going to take a moment to reflect on my process and understand why I was making these mistakes. I’m committed to getting this right!

Unfortunately, if I change to another similar question, it still sums up the numbers wrong.
