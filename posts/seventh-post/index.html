Self reflection: looking for jobs in hk, learning AI and life

2025年12月15日 阅读时间：4 分钟

Wayne's Blog • Self-Reflection

2 interviews out of 100 job application 链接到标题

As a former quantum physics research postdoc, I’m looking for jobs in industry to survive in Hong Kong. I start job application on 16 Oct 2025. With not much eager to find a job in this year (some part of me inside still want to gap), only around 100 CVs are submitted, which contains lots of my physics research technical terms because my experience is primarily in research, not industrial settings. Most of the job application are about quant finance or data analysis.

It turns out that I got 2 interviews only. The first one needs me to know how to operate and maintain the new quantitative trading system written by oursourcing. I said I can do it but the company said they need to wait some time before the outsourcing system is finished. I realized I was unprepared for the second one. The second one wants me to sell their AI trading product, which I don’t know in the first place. I thought it’s about building the product but it’s not.

Actionable lessons 链接到标题
  • Edit my CV to a project-orient one, which is more specific to the job I want to apply.
  • I don’t like a job that needs to sell something and talk very much about something I don’t know. I will try to apply for a data analyst job. Learning Google data analyst course is a good start.

Learn AI 链接到标题

It starts with my second interview looking for a job in hk. I want to record my interview so that I can learn some from it. My idea is to deploy an AI model in my Macbook Air to transcribe the audio and use another AI like Gemma3 to sumarize the text. I need it to be free and useful. I chose an open-source LLM called Whisper. For a min audio, the transcribed text is quite precise and it takes 10 seconds. However, for a 25 mins audio, it takes half hour with some repeated nonsense words. The solution for quick and clear transcription is either buying a better computer with GPU or pre-edit the audio to make it more clear and extract the people-voice part.

Lesson 链接到标题

Another problem occurs where I want to distinguish “who is talking what”. I find an article explaining how to build a “who is talking what” system with Whisper and pyannote.audio. This is a good technical article which first answers why use these tools and what to build, then explains how to build such a system with analogy clearly instead of mysterious technical language, and finally explores some options to extend the system. Basically, it involves the problem-solving mindset with clear and understandable language, which I should mimic.

Life 链接到标题

Recently I watched several suspense dramas. I can get joy out of the process of finding the truth. AI can’t replace human to generate good ideas, can they?

I realize that maybe I switched my focus on my daily life too much. My girlfriend gave me a dailyplanner notebook. I try to write down maximum 3 to-do list and execute it, with the awareness that try to focus on one thing at least 20 mins. With more focus I may do things with less friction. The better I feel, the more likely I will keep doing it. Simple way to build a good habit.

I tend to approach problems through action, while my girlfriend prefers a more reflective process. I learn things from doing first and thinking later while my girlfriend is the other way around. I like to write down thoughts to help me clarify the thinking. I should be aware that I have the best partner around me that I can learn thinking from.

At last, I want to recommend this article called How to Cultivate Autonomy. The author is a PE teacher. She discussed that playing is the nature of kids while we adults may set too many boundaries to the kids that they choose not to play.

With teens, and groups of them, things get tricky. They are so socially driven and protective. Unless they get placed in a group that happens to have a peer leader of greater skill and ability, the guys will generally be goofy and the girls will generally be complacent. It is a way to deflect trying and failing.

This mirrors observations I’ve made about the importance of pushing people slightly out of their comfort zones from my basketball coaching experience. She shows how she nudge those who are idle and afraid to move towards changing and express themselves. The article starts with the young, but applicapable with all the human beings.
